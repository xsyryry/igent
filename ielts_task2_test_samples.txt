【样例 1】政府类

【题目】
Improvements in health, education and trade are essential for the development of poor nations. However, the governments of richer nations should take more responsibility for helping poor nations in such areas.
To what extent do you agree or disagree?

【Band 9 样例（按原文件整理）】
There is clear evidence across the world that a gulf has emerged between the richest and poorest nations of the globe. The richest 1% of the world's population hold an astonishing 50% of the world's wealth. In a time of economic hardship in many parts of the world, the time has surely come for a new economic model, with a progressive form of taxation at its heart.

The economist John Maynard Keynes, architect of Roosevelt's New Deal in the 1930s, advocated an economic system of tight controls on the supply of money, to ensure the fair distribution of wealth throughout society. This model was abandoned during the 1980s by governments in the western world. Following the economic crash of 2008, and the subsequent sluggish recovery experienced in many countries, it is surely time to re-evaluate the kind of economic model we elect to dominate society.

Marx argued that capitalism is by its very nature exploitative. Given the widening gap between rich and poor, it is high time the economists of the world re-acquainted themselves with the German economist's magnum opus, Das Kapital. Governments must take a more interventionist approach, of the kind propounded by Marx, to tackle the alarming disparity between the richest and poorest in the world. It is no use one country adopting a socialist economic agenda; collective action across the whole world is required.

Therefore, by way of conclusion, I have no doubt that the richest should shoulder a heavier economic burden, in order to alleviate the suffering of the poor, thus creating a fairer, more egalitarian society.

【Band 7 样例（测试用）】
Poor countries often struggle because they lack money, technology and stable institutions, so it is reasonable to argue that richer governments should do more to support them. I largely agree with this view, although such support should be practical and long-term rather than simply consisting of short bursts of aid.

The main reason wealthy nations should help is that many poor countries cannot improve healthcare, education and trade systems on their own. Building hospitals, training teachers and upgrading ports all require huge investment. In some African countries, for example, even basic medicine is not always available in rural areas. If richer governments share funding and expertise, the effects can be significant. Better vaccination systems reduce disease, stronger schools create a more skilled population, and fairer trade access allows local businesses to grow. These are not acts of charity alone; they also make the world more stable.

Another point is that rich nations have often benefited more from the current global economic system, so they have a stronger responsibility to address inequality. Multinational companies can profit from cheap labour and raw materials in developing nations, while local communities receive only limited benefits. For that reason, aid should not be limited to donations. It should also include debt relief, fairer trade rules and support for local industry. Otherwise, poorer countries may remain dependent for decades.

However, assistance must be carefully designed. Some foreign aid programmes fail because money is badly managed or because donors impose solutions that do not match local needs. Therefore, richer countries should work with local governments and communities instead of treating them as passive receivers.

In conclusion, I agree that wealthy governments ought to take more responsibility for helping poorer nations, but their support must focus on sustainable development rather than temporary generosity.

【Band 7 预期扣分点】
- 论证总体清楚，但例子较泛，缺少更具体的数据或案例支撑。
- 第二段和第三段有一定重复：都在说“富国有责任帮助穷国”，展开深度略有限。
- 词汇准确但不够灵活，表达偏稳妥，冲击高分的语言亮点不足。
- 属于“有观点、有解释，但论证深度和例证精度略弱”的典型 7 分档测试文。

【Band 5 样例（测试用）】
I think rich countries should help poor countries because poor countries have many problems. They need money for schools, hospitals and trade, so rich countries can give them support. I agree with this opinion, but I do not think rich countries should do everything.

First of all, poor countries usually do not have enough money. Because of this, people cannot get good education and medical care. If rich countries give money, these problems can become smaller. Also, rich countries are stronger and they have more experience, so they can show poor countries how to develop business and industry. This is good for everyone and it can make the world better.

On the other hand, I think some poor countries also need to help themselves. If they always wait for aid, they may become dependent. Sometimes governments use money in a bad way, and then the help does not really reach normal people. So giving money is not enough and it is not always successful.

In conclusion, I agree that rich nations should take more responsibility, especially in health and education. However, poor countries should also make efforts by themselves. If both sides work together, there will be more progress.

【Band 5 预期扣分点】
- 篇幅偏短，Task Response 展开不足。
- 观点较简单，论证链短，例子缺失。
- 连接手段单一，段落推进比较机械。
- 词汇和句式明显偏简单，难以支撑高 LR/GRA。
- 属于“基本回应题目，但发展不足、语言资源有限”的 5 分档测试文。

【样例 2】旅游类

【题目】
More attention is paid to being responsible tourists in order to preserve the cultural and environmental aspects of tourist places. However, some people think it is impossible to be a 'responsible tourist' in modern society.
To what extent do you agree or disagree?

【Band 9 样例（按原文件整理）】
Responsible tourism is a concept that has arisen only in the last few years, with the idea that all visitors to popular scenic and historical locations are respectful towards local customs and the environment threatened by an increasing number of people who demonstrate a flagrant lack of regard for an area's natural beauty, despoiling it with rubbish and so forth.

Everyone shares a collective responsibility to ensure they treat the global environment with the utmost respect and care. Tragically, large numbers of tourists, both domestic and those from overseas, completely disregard regulations designed to govern behaviour in such places. This development is utterly unacceptable and must be addressed with harsh penalties for rule breakers. There is simply no excuse for this kind of errant behaviour and contemptuous attitude towards the natural environment.

I do not believe for one moment that it is impossible for tourists to behave responsibly in contemporary society. We have reached a period in history of tremendous technological development and more people have access to education than ever before. For a species that gave rise to the likes of ethical and moral theorists like Hume, Kant and Sartre, we cannot abnegate responsibility for individual behaviour by making spurious arguments about large populations being too difficult to manage. A system of penalties for abusers, properly and sensibly enforced by authorities, would go some way to addressing this problem.

I am in broad agreement with the first of the two statements, but unreservedly refute the second claim. It is unambiguously the case that more can be done to ensure tourists behave responsibly and that stiff penalties are the solution.

【Band 7 样例（测试用）】
Although mass tourism can damage local culture and the natural environment, I disagree with the view that being a responsible tourist is impossible in modern society. It is difficult, especially when travel has become cheap and highly commercialised, but both individuals and authorities can still reduce harm in meaningful ways.

Responsible tourism is clearly achievable at the individual level. Many travellers already choose public transport, avoid littering, respect dress codes at religious sites and support local businesses rather than international chains. These actions may seem small, but when repeated by thousands of visitors they can make a considerable difference. In historical cities such as Kyoto or Florence, for instance, signs and visitor guides now explain how tourists should behave in sensitive areas. When people are informed in advance, they are more likely to act responsibly.

At the same time, governments and tourist companies must admit that responsibility cannot depend only on personal morality. Popular destinations are often promoted aggressively, while little is done to control visitor numbers or waste. In that situation, even well-meaning tourists may contribute to overcrowding and pollution. Therefore, authorities should introduce clearer rules, such as limiting entry to fragile sites, charging environmental fees and punishing destructive behaviour. Travel platforms could also educate customers before their trip instead of merely advertising attractive photos.

Admittedly, not every tourist will behave well, and some people continue to ignore local customs. However, this does not prove that responsible tourism is impossible. It simply shows that the system needs stronger management.

In conclusion, modern tourism makes responsible behaviour more challenging, but it is still realistic. With better education and stricter regulation, tourists can enjoy destinations without destroying what makes them valuable.

【Band 7 预期扣分点】
- 立场明确，但对“为什么有人认为不可能”这一反方逻辑回应略短。
- 例子合理但不够深入，更多是常识性说明。
- 连接自然但略偏套路化，缺少更复杂的论证层次。
- 属于“整体完成较好，但双边讨论深度略不均衡”的 7 分档测试文。

【Band 5 样例（测试用）】
Nowadays many people travel to many places, so being a responsible tourist is an important topic. I think it is possible to be responsible, but in modern society it is not easy.

First, tourists can do some simple things. They can put rubbish in bins, follow local rules and do not damage old buildings. They can also buy local food and products, which helps local people. If tourists do these things, tourism will be better for cultural places and natural places.

However, there are also many problems. Some tourists only want to take photos and have fun. They make noise, throw rubbish and sometimes wear unsuitable clothes in religious places. Also, too many tourists come to famous places because tickets are cheap and social media makes these places popular. As a result, even good tourists can create pressure on the environment.

So I think responsible tourism is possible, but governments should make more rules. For example, they can fine people who damage places and they can tell tourists what to do. Hotels and travel companies should also give advice.

In conclusion, I do not agree that responsible tourism is impossible. It is possible, but many people still do not do it, so stronger control is needed.

【Band 5 预期扣分点】
- 回应题目基本到位，但分析较表面。
- 论点和例子较简单，缺少深入解释。
- 表达重复明显，如 responsible / tourists / places 高频重复。
- 句式比较单一，复杂句控制有限。
- 属于“观点清楚但发展不足、语言偏简单”的 5 分档测试文。

【样例 3】媒体类

【题目】
Today, the high sales of popular consumer goods reflect the power of advertising and not the real needs of the society in which they are sold.
To what extent do you agree or disagree?

【Band 9 样例（按原文件整理）】
Advertising and marketing are pivotal components of the sale and distribution of goods; without attractive marketing campaigns and vivid adverts, companies would be less competitive and sell fewer products. Obviously, advertising is not the primary motivating factor behind a person's decision to purchase a product; other considerations must be taken into account.

People purchase goods and services for a wide array of reasons. Necessity is the basic impulse that determines whether someone buys a specific item. Food is bought not because it is a luxury item, but because it serves a basic human need. Similarly, clothes and shelter are bought because these are the most essential components of an individual's existence. One might also argue that in today's technologically advanced society, gadgets and devices such as mobile phones, computers and the like are equally essential to one's everyday activities, particularly with regard to work.

It is evident, though, that advertising has a tremendous impact on consumers' purchasing decisions in today's market-driven economy. If one takes food as an example, advertising can have a great effect on the type of food one buys. Different brands of orange juice, priced and advertised according to different strategies, will obviously occupy varying shares of the market. Advertising can go a considerable way to determining the scale of distribution and success of a product once it goes on the market.

【Band 7 样例（测试用）】
I partly agree that the popularity of many consumer goods is driven by advertising rather than genuine social needs. Advertising is extremely powerful in shaping what people want, but it would be inaccurate to say that real needs play no role at all.

There is no doubt that advertising can create demand where none previously existed. Modern campaigns do more than present information; they associate products with status, beauty or success. A person may buy the newest phone not because their old one is unusable, but because advertisements suggest that owning the latest model is a sign of ambition or modernity. This is especially true for cosmetics, fashion items and luxury goods, where branding often matters more than practical value. In such cases, high sales figures reflect marketing strength more than public necessity.

However, the statement becomes less convincing when we consider essential or widely useful products. Food, medicine, household appliances and digital devices are bought because they serve clear purposes in daily life. Although companies still advertise these items heavily, their sales cannot be explained by promotion alone. For example, people need laptops for work and study, regardless of how eye-catching a commercial may be. Advertising may influence which brand consumers choose, but not the underlying need to buy the item.

Overall, advertising has become powerful enough to shape tastes and even create artificial desires. Nevertheless, popular consumer goods are not sold in a social vacuum. In many cases, their success reflects a combination of real need and persuasive promotion.

In conclusion, I disagree with the statement in its absolute form. Advertising is a major force behind sales, but genuine needs still remain an essential part of consumer behaviour.

【Band 7 预期扣分点】
- 有清晰立场，但“社会真实需求”这一概念界定不够深入。
- 论证比较稳妥，缺少更鲜明或更有分析力的例子。
- 语言准确但偏常规，词汇多样性中上而非非常强。
- 属于“部分同意类题目完成不错，但思想深度略有限”的 7 分档测试文。

【Band 5 样例（测试用）】
I think advertising is very powerful today, so I mostly agree that popular goods sell well because of ads. Many people buy things they do not really need.

First, companies spend a lot of money on advertising. They put adverts on TV, phones and public places, so people see products again and again. Because of this, people may feel they need to buy new shoes, new phones or expensive drinks. In fact, sometimes their old things are still fine. This shows that advertising can change people's mind.

However, I do not think all goods are sold only because of ads. Some things are really necessary, such as food, medicine and clothes. People need them in normal life, so they will buy them anyway. Advertising may help one company sell more than another company, but it does not create all needs.

In conclusion, advertising is a big reason for the success of many consumer goods, especially fashionable items. But real needs are still important for some products, so the statement is not fully true.

【Band 5 预期扣分点】
- 观点较清楚，但发展不充分。
- 例子泛泛，没有具体分析广告如何影响不同商品。
- 段落结构简单，论证链较短。
- 词汇和句式范围有限，重复较多。
- 属于“有基本回应，但论证与语言资源偏弱”的 5 分档测试文。

【样例 4】环境类

【题目】
In the modern world, it is no longer necessary to use animals for food or use animal products, for instance, clothing and medicines.
To what extent do you agree or disagree?

【Band 9 样例（按原文件整理）】
Animal rights groups like PETA (People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals) have long argued against the exploitation of animals and the testing of chemical products on animals. Whether we should test products on animals is a moot point, given the fact that so few companies do this nowadays.

Nevertheless, the moral questions pertaining to this are still worth exploring. While it may no longer be necessary to test products on animals, there are still instances where animals are used to test human innovations. Medicine is one such area where animal testing is still regarded as essential to the development of drugs and other pioneering treatments for incurable diseases, such as Alzheimer's disease, cancer and AIDS. While human test agents are used for some courses of treatment, there are inherent dangers associated with testing on humans.

A recent case in the UK arose where a drug company tested a controversial drug on humans and several died following an adverse reaction, while another individual was left permanently paralysed. In such cases, animals are perhaps better test agents than humans. While some may find it unpalatable, if an animal suffers any sort of lasting disability that can lead to a destruction of their quality of life, they can still be legally euthanised to end their suffering. Humans cannot be assisted in suicide and the issue continues to arouse great contention.

I would conclude that animal testing is still necessary in fields like medicine, where the risks of testing on humans are too perilous to consider.

【Band 7 样例（测试用）】
I disagree with the idea that animals and animal products are no longer necessary in the modern world. Although people now have more alternatives than before, it is still unrealistic to claim that all uses of animals can be completely replaced at present.

In terms of food and clothing, the statement is partly convincing. Many people can live healthily on plant-based diets, and synthetic materials are now widely available for shoes, jackets and other products. In wealthy urban societies especially, people often eat meat or wear leather more out of preference than necessity. For this reason, reducing animal consumption is both possible and desirable, particularly when factory farming causes environmental damage and animal suffering.

However, the argument is much weaker when it comes to medicine. Despite advances in laboratory technology, some drugs and treatments still need to be tested in living organisms before they can be considered safe for humans. Cell cultures and computer simulations are helpful, but they cannot yet reproduce the full complexity of a body. If animal testing were abandoned immediately, medical progress in areas such as cancer treatment or vaccine development could slow down. In this field, the issue is not simple convenience but risk management and public safety.

That said, necessity should not be used as an excuse for unlimited exploitation. Governments should fund alternatives, strengthen welfare regulations and ban animal use wherever reliable substitutes already exist.

In conclusion, I do not agree that animal use is no longer necessary in all areas. It has become less necessary in food and clothing, but medicine remains a significant exception.

【Band 7 预期扣分点】
- 回应全面，但食品、服装、医药三部分的展开比重略不均衡，药品部分更强。
- 例子偏概括化，缺少更具体的实例支撑。
- 语言较自然，但少数句型重复，学术表达层次感一般。
- 属于“立场明确、覆盖面较好，但论证深度不够均衡”的 7 分档测试文。

【Band 5 样例（测试用）】
I disagree with this statement because animals are still used in many parts of life. It is true that people can choose other food and materials now, but animals are still important.

First, many people still eat meat, eggs and milk because they believe these foods are useful and normal. Also, some clothes like wool or leather are strong and warm. Artificial materials exist, but they are not always better or cheaper. So it is difficult to say animal products are not necessary at all.

Second, medicines are an important reason. Before using a new medicine on humans, scientists need to know if it is safe. Animals are often used for this purpose. If they are not used, there may be more danger for people. Of course, scientists should try to reduce animal testing, but stopping it completely is not realistic now.

In conclusion, I think the statement is too extreme. Modern society has more choices than before, but animals and animal products are still necessary in some fields.

【Band 5 预期扣分点】
- 回应了题目，但思想发展较简单。
- 食品、服装和医药都提到了，但展开很浅。
- 连接方式单一，论证层次有限。
- 词汇较基础，复杂句使用少。
- 属于“覆盖任务但展开不足”的 5 分档测试文。

【样例 5】社会类

【题目】
Some people believe famous people's support towards international aid organisations draws attention to problems, while others think celebrities make the problems less important.
Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

【Band 9 样例（按原文件整理）】
There are many conflicting views about celebrity involvement in charitable causes and high-profile public campaigns. Some argue that the presence of high-profile figures in campaigns to draw attention to major issues such as war, poverty and famine draws greater attention to the issues and increases public impetus to call for more action. Others, though, regard celebrities as essentially PR-hungry sycophants who simply attach their names to causes to generate good publicity for themselves.

Celebrity involvement in notable causes can be traced back to the 1985 Live Aid concert, where internationally renowned musicians gathered at Wembley Stadium in London to perform music and raise money for poverty-stricken nations across the world. In the UK, initiatives such as Comic Relief and Children In Need have also relied upon celebrity involvement to raise awareness. Such events certainly have the effect of raising large amounts of money and increasing awareness of major problems blighting the world.

Nonetheless, many celebrities choose to involve themselves in charitable works because they have been advised by publicists that it will either increase their profile or enable them to better promote their latest album or film. While this may appear benign, there is surely a moral objection to this kind of cynical behaviour. Money may be raised from their involvement, but one must question the ethics of someone trying to exploit others' suffering to enrich themselves even further.

Overall, I would argue that celebrity involvement in worthy causes can be a good thing, but I do not believe that those who seek to further their own careers by doing so ought to be held up as beacons of charity.

【Band 7 样例（测试用）】
Celebrities often appear in campaigns for global poverty, disaster relief and refugee support. Some people argue that this is beneficial because famous individuals can attract attention that charities would never gain on their own, while others believe celebrity participation turns serious issues into entertainment. In my view, celebrity support is useful overall, although its value depends on how responsibly it is handled.

Those who support celebrity involvement point out that visibility matters. International aid organisations compete for public attention in a crowded media environment, and famous actors, athletes or singers can immediately draw audiences to urgent crises. A campaign fronted by a well-known person is more likely to be reported by the press, shared on social media and remembered by the public. In addition, celebrities can encourage donations from people who would not normally engage with politics or humanitarian issues. In this sense, fame becomes a communication tool.

On the other hand, critics are right to worry that celebrities may oversimplify serious problems. Humanitarian crises are often caused by war, corruption or long-term inequality, yet campaigns sometimes reduce them to emotional images and slogans. This can create sympathy without understanding. In some cases, the celebrity becomes the centre of attention, while the affected communities remain invisible. If famous supporters are using charity mainly to improve their image, the campaign may appear shallow or even disrespectful.

Overall, I believe celebrity involvement can do more good than harm, provided that it supports experts rather than replacing them. Charities should use public figures to attract attention, but the real message must still come from aid workers and the people directly affected.

In conclusion, celebrities can increase awareness and donations, but campaigns should be designed carefully so that public attention remains focused on the problem itself.

【Band 7 预期扣分点】
- 双边讨论完整，但对反方的“让问题变得不重要”分析还可以更深入。
- 例子较概括，没有具体 campaign 的细致分析。
- 语言稳定，但部分表达偏常规，观点深刻性略逊于高分文。
- 属于“讨论两边较平衡、观点清楚，但分析力度稍弱”的 7 分档测试文。

【Band 5 样例（测试用）】
Famous people often support charities and international aid groups. Some people think this is good because it helps more people know about problems. Others think celebrities make the problems less important. I think both ideas are true, but overall famous people are helpful.

On one hand, celebrities can attract public attention very quickly. If a famous singer or actor talks about hunger or war, many people will watch the news and maybe donate money. This is useful because many charities do not have enough power by themselves. A famous face can make people notice a problem.

On the other hand, some celebrities only want attention for themselves. They may take photos with poor children and post them online, but they do not really understand the situation. In this case, the public may focus more on the celebrity than on the real problem. Also, some campaigns become too simple and emotional.

In my opinion, celebrity support is still positive because aid groups need attention and money. However, famous people should act carefully and not become the main story.

In conclusion, celebrities can help international aid organisations, but if they behave in a selfish way, they can also reduce the seriousness of the issue.

【Band 5 预期扣分点】
- 完成了 discuss both views 任务，但两边展开都较浅。
- 例子缺失，支持力度不足。
- 观点明确但分析不够细，论证较直白。
- 语言资源和句式范围偏简单。
- 属于“任务完成基本合格，但发展不足”的 5 分档测试文。

【样例 6】教育类

【题目】
Some experts believe that it is better for children to begin learning a foreign language at primary school rather than secondary school.
Do the advantages of this outweigh the disadvantages?

【Band 9 样例（按原文件整理）】
Language learning is an art form in the eyes of some. Research indicates that children with a proficiency in music are more likely to master a second language than those who have few artistic gifts. Moreover, large numbers of primary schools across the world have now recognised the importance of providing students with the opportunity to learn a language different from their native tongue, with Mandarin replacing French as the principal second language in many European countries.

The earlier a child begins learning a new language, the higher the rate at which they acquire vocabulary and language structures will be. Even one 40-minute session each day will accelerate the speed of language acquisition, while also providing the child with transferable skills that can be applied to other areas of learning. Some would argue that if children learn a new language at elementary level, other skills and developmental attributes may be neglected, such as physical education or the absorption of critical social skills.

I do not adhere to this view and believe that children should be encouraged to study a second language at an early age. There are no insurmountable obstacles to achieving this and there is no evidence that this would have a detrimental impact on other areas of a child's growth or academic achievement.

In an increasingly global nexus, it is more vital than ever before for the people of the world to interact and communicate with each other. To that end, teaching primary school children a language other than their native tongue is the best approach to this. Not only will they acquire the tools of communication on the international stage, they will also have the opportunity to embrace the impenetrable mysteries of a new culture.

【Band 7 样例（测试用）】
Starting a foreign language in primary school is often seen as a better option than waiting until secondary education. In my opinion, the advantages do outweigh the disadvantages, although the success of such a policy depends on how the lessons are designed.

The main benefit of earlier language learning is that young children are generally more open to new sounds and patterns. They are less afraid of making mistakes and can often imitate pronunciation more naturally than older learners. Early exposure can therefore build confidence and help students develop a long-term familiarity with another language. In addition, language study at a young age may improve memory, listening skills and cultural awareness, which are useful beyond the language classroom itself.

There are, however, some disadvantages. Primary school timetables are already crowded, and adding a foreign language may reduce time for literacy, numeracy or physical education. Another problem is that not all schools have qualified teachers, especially in poorer regions. If lessons are badly taught or focus too much on memorisation, children may lose interest before they reach secondary school. In that case, an early start alone will not guarantee success.

Even so, I believe these drawbacks are manageable. A foreign language does not need to dominate the curriculum; short, enjoyable lessons can be enough to create a foundation. Schools can also use songs, games and simple interaction rather than test-heavy methods. If children begin early and continue consistently, the long-term gains are likely to be greater than the temporary costs.

In conclusion, although there are challenges related to time and teaching quality, the benefits of beginning a foreign language at primary school clearly outweigh the disadvantages.

【Band 7 预期扣分点】
- 立场明确，但 disadvantages 部分展开略少，论证重心明显偏向 advantages。
- 例子偏概括，缺少具体教育场景支撑。
- 语言准确自然，但表达略稳，缺少更高阶的复杂性。

There is no doubt that age is a major determinant of people's spending habits and one's shopping choices are subject to change as one gets older.

Other factors, though, contribute to the spending habits of individuals. Social background, education level, gender, race and other specific indicators have an impact on a person's spending habits. There are a wide range of factors that affect people's spending habits among which age is certainly important, but it is by no means the most important factor.
